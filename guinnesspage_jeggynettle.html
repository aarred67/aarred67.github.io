The Jeggy Nettle

Located in the Holylands of Belfast

I’ve steadily decided that nothing can beat the Jeggy Nettle. What a place. This was suggested to us by our cab driver on our way to a one act play about a couple sending their last goodbyes to friends and families during the apocalypse—a metaphor for the COVID-19 pandemic, which became rather obvious once facetime was the only form of contact. Though a rather interesting play, the bar made this night the best one out of all my evenings in Ireland.

Entering the pub, you’d have thought there were absolutely no seats available. Each table had a group of friends or lovers sitting across from each other, playing a game of cards or sharing stories accompanied by hearty laughs. As I did a quick lap around the pub, I was able to grab a table that was packed only a minute before. Better yet, it was located right next to a dying fire in the chimney… and directly in front of a live musician who oddly looked identical to Lou Reed. It was a beautiful setting with the best little spot to do some people watching while I enjoyed a Guinness. This will absolutely be my first spot I return to the next time I’m in Belfast!
